Cairo University Faculty of Engineering was established in 1905, making it one of the oldest engineering faculties in Egypt and the Arab world.

The faculty is located in Giza, on the main campus of Cairo University along the Nile.

The faculty offers undergraduate programs in departments including Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Architecture, and Systems & Biomedical Engineering.

Undergraduate study takes 5 years to complete, ending with a Bachelor of Science in Engineering degree.

The faculty also offers postgraduate programs including Master's and PhD degrees across all departments.

Admission to the faculty is based on the Egyptian Thanaweyya Amma certificate, with high minimum scores required due to its competitive nature.

The Computer Engineering department covers topics such as algorithms, computer architecture, operating systems, networks, and software engineering.

The Electrical Engineering department focuses on power systems, electronics, communications, and control systems.

The Civil Engineering department covers structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, hydraulics, and transportation.

The faculty has several research centers including the Center for Renewable Energy and the Center for Environmental Engineering.

Students are required to complete a graduation project in their final year, which is evaluated by a committee of professors.

The faculty library contains thousands of engineering references, journals, and research papers available to students and staff.

The academic year is divided into two semesters: the first runs from October to January, and the second from February to June.

Cairo University Faculty of Engineering is accredited by the National Authority for Quality Assurance and Accreditation of Education (NAQAAE).
